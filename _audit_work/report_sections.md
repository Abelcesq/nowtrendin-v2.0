

## executive_summary

Bottom line: NowTrendIn's instrument partially reflects human attention today, but it FAILS the no-gap test on the data's most important live data point and on a systematic class of already-arrived topics. On 2026-06-22 Alan Greenspan died at 100 — the day's premier, globally wall-to-wall mainstream event — and the engine scored it Detection 91.8 / Confidence 75.6 / gap +16.2, the single largest positive gap in the 100-topic sample, landing a confirmed obituary in the "very early, not-yet-confirmed" Anomalies band. That is precisely the broken behavior the product's own no-gap principle says invalidates the gap metric. The failure is systemic, not a fluke: every mainstream nation and mega-cap outside a privileged top tier collapses to a near-identical ~45 Detection / ~67 Confidence signature badged EMERGING (china, india, russia, france, apple, openai, korea), with a uniform ~-22 LAGGING gap — Confidence systematically exceeding Detection on topics that have unambiguously already arrived, the inverse of the intended "early = Detection leads" design. The single biggest risk is therefore credibility at the already-arrived end of the curve: the dual-pathway anti-collapse gate demonstrably works at the very top (trump 94.6/94.2, FIFA 93.7/93.7, iran 86.1/90.1, election 94.7/94.3) but does not extend across the full mainstream band. Separately, a real data-integrity bug exists: the stored heisenberg_gap field contradicts its own Detection−Confidence on the entire mcp family (det−conf ≈ +1.5 but stored gap −23), directly breaking the "both scores explain each other" test. The good news that must be weighed against this: the scores DO discriminate (monotonic stage ladder, 520/595 distinct values, gap converging to ~0 only for genuinely-arrived BREAKOUT topics), the engine does NOT fabricate confidence for niche topics (a one-directional failure mode), and the no-N-into-Gradient firewall — the core integrity rule — is correctly enforced in live code. The headline recommendation: do NOT make any public "before it arrives" lead claim until (a) a non-circular Wikipedia+GDELT no-gap anchor is wired as a held-out validator and the mainstream-collapse + gap-incoherence bugs are fixed, and (b) the accuracy ledger clears its product-success bar (the moat charter's own bar is currently CONTRADICTED at 0.0% honest hit rate). The instrument is promising and structurally honest in its design, but it is not yet a defensible, ledger-backed "human attention" instrument across the full diffusion curve. Caveat on scope: this audit rests on a 100-topic no-gap sample within a 2,103-topic snapshot and an 88-instrument market pull, all single-day (2026-06-22/23) — directionally decisive but not a multi-week out-of-sample validation.


## what_it_is

The Gradient Score is an "attention intelligence" instrument that emits TWO headline numbers from ONE measurement set, built on an explicit (not metaphorical) Heisenberg trade-off: a trend's POSITION on the diffusion curve (how early/niche it is) and its MOMENTUM (whether it will sustain) cannot both be measured at maximum precision at once, because waiting to confirm momentum is the act of measurement that destroys earliness. DETECTION is the earliness-optimized score (accepts ~22% false positives, audience: creators/marketers); CONFIDENCE is the precision-optimized score (target <9% FP, audience: institutional analysts/investors). The GAP = Detection − Confidence is treated as the single most precise read of where a topic sits on the curve: a large positive gap = detected early, not yet broadly confirmed; the gap closes UPWARD as a genuine trend matures (both converge HIGH = "confirmed, act now") and closes DOWNWARD as a false positive collapses (both fall LOW). Displayed STAGES are Detection-derived with exact cutoffs: BREAKOUT ≥85, STRONG 70–84, EMERGING 55–69 (shown to users as "INDICATING" — display-only rename, internal key stays EMERGING), MARGINAL/WATCHING 35–54, MONITORING <35; ANOMALY is an orthogonal flag, not a strength band, firing when Detection runs 16+ points ahead of Confidence. A THIRD score, N (nowtrendin_score), measures internal in-app query demand and is the ranking key for the "Now TrendIn" view (while "All Signals" ranks by Detection); critically, N is EXCLUDED from the Gradient Score itself to prevent a reflexive search→score→search feedback loop. DOC-vs-CODE reconciliation, with drift flagged: (1) the docs/CLAUDE.md and even in-code comments still cite headline weights of G 0.40 / D 0.25 / I 0.20 / M 0.10 / C 0.05 for Detection and an N weight of 10–12% — these are STALE; live code runs a six-component (G/I/M/D/C/P, plus Persistence) N-excluded set renormalized to 1.0, with N at 0.0 everywhere. (2) METHODOLOGY.md names the displayed bands "BREAKOUT/STRONG/EMERGING/WATCHING/MONITORING" while signals.ts uses MARGINAL as the primary 35–54 label and renames EMERGING→INDICATING — a minor doc lag. (3) "Overall" is defined two different ways: weighted-six at scoring time vs (Detection+Confidence)/2 at serve/calibration time, so /scores and /detail can diverge. (4) The legacy five-component gradient_engine_backend.py (no P, no N) is NOT the serve path and must not be cited as authoritative; the canonical live scorer is gravitational_anomaly_detector.py.


## factors_and_weighting

The score is built from six external measurement components, each scored 0–100. INTENT (May-2026 Heisenberg PDF, faithfully built in the legacy 5-component module): G — Gradient Strength (niche-to-mainstream engagement density ratio; fires instantly from ~8 signals); I — Inertia (consecutive 6h windows of accelerating signal count; highest-latency, best sustain predictor); M — Medium/Platform diversity (cross-platform diffusion pattern match); D — Dark Matter (probabilistic hidden-private-conversation inference from first-timer ratio, engagement asymmetry, vocabulary clustering); C — Confidence Decay (freshness/age, Heisenberg-exempt). The live canonical engine adds P — Persistence (cycles held above EMERGING). LIVE CODE WEIGHTS (renormalized six-component, N=0): Detection = G 0.375 + D 0.216 + I 0.182 + M 0.102 + C 0.057 + P 0.068; Confidence = I 0.278 + P 0.267 + M 0.222 + G 0.122 + C 0.067 + D 0.044; Overall = G 0.244 + I 0.222 + M 0.167 + D 0.133 + C 0.078 + P 0.156. The structure is faithful to intent (Detection leads on the earliest-firing G+D; Confidence leads on the slowest-to-confirm I+P+M), but WEIGHT DRIFT is real and material: published/comment weights (G 0.40, N 10–12%) do not match live constants, and the in-code comment block at apply_calibration lines 1100–1101 lists weights that match NEITHER the docs NOR the code below it. DUAL-PATHWAY: every topic is scored on the expert G·I·M·D·C·P composite by default, then a dual-pathway blend runs last with weight w = mainstream_ratio (baseline-relative deviation from the topic's OWN 12-cycle footprint). Mainstream-tier evidence lands in G's DENOMINATOR (suppresses the early read) and raises w; expert/niche tier keeps w≈0 and preserves the expert gradient (the moat). This is the correct design — fame ≠ movement — and it visibly protects the top tier (FIFA would otherwise collapse to 27.6). WHERE WEIGHTING IS SUSPECT: (1) the same-class ESTABLISHED discount has THREE different multipliers depending on maturity source (0.25 / 0.40 / 0.45) — a calibration inconsistency. (2) Overall and Stage are computed on different inputs at scoring vs serve time, so a topic can carry two different stages. (3) The mainstream-collapse evidence (see error analysis) shows the dual-pathway/calibration preservation is NOT firing for mid-mainstream rows. CIRCULARITY ASSESSMENT: the headline integrity rule is upheld — N (in-app demand) is verifiably excluded from all three composites, so there is NO forbidden internal-feedback leak; ranking-by-N is a display choice that does not contaminate the score. Dark Matter uses external-platform (Reddit) first-timer/asymmetry signals, NOT NowTrendIn's own users — acceptable. The ONE real concern: PERSISTENCE is self-referential — it is derived from topic_lifecycle counters built from the engine's own prior OVERALL scores crossing the EMERGING threshold, and P then feeds Overall (0.156) and Confidence (0.267). Inputs G/I/M/D/C are independent, so it is feedback rather than pure circularity, but P is NOT an input independent of what it validates — the classic violation of the project's own "validator must use independent inputs" standard, and a standing item to watch.


## error_analysis

This is where the instrument is weakest. THE DECISIVE FAILURE (basis: WebSearch-confirmed against the Federal Reserve official statement + CBS/NBC/ABC): Alan Greenspan died 2026-06-22 at 100 — the day's #1 confirmed mainstream story — and scored Detection 91.8 / Confidence 75.6 / gap +16.2, the sole large positive gap in the sample and squarely in the Anomalies band. The no-gap principle explicitly defines this exact case (an obviously-mainstream, fully-confirmed topic read as early/uncertain) as the broken state that invalidates the gap metric. The likely mechanism: Detection is correctly high, but Persistence has not accrued for a one-day-old event, depressing Confidence — the documented "gap closes as P rises" lifecycle — but for a wall-to-wall confirmed obituary the instrument must not read it as a speculative lead. This needs a same-surge/breaking-news check. THE SYSTEMATIC FAILURE (basis: internal distribution + footprint counts): seven mainstream nations/mega-caps collapse to a near-identical signature — china 45.6/67.5 (219 mentions/11 platforms), india 45.6/67.5, russia 45.5/67.3, france 45.3/67.5, apple 45.2/67.2, openai 45.4/67.1, korea 45.4/67.1 — all badged EMERGING with a uniform ~-22 LAGGING gap; obama is worse (24.3/56.7, WATCHING, gap -32.4). Confidence systematically exceeds Detection on already-arrived topics, the inverse of the intended design, indicating Detection is being clamped below what the footprint justifies and that the dual-pathway preservation gate is not firing for mid-mainstream rows. CONFIRMED ERROR (adversarially verified, severity medium): "strait of hormuz" reads Detection 23.9 / WATCHING while the shorter "hormuz" token reads STRONG 72.3/83.2 — the SAME story (the dominant 2026 oil/geopolitics flashpoint, IEA-described as the largest supply disruption in oil-market history). Root cause verified in code: normalize_topic() only lowercases/strips punctuation and there is no alias-consolidation layer for non-AI topics, so surface-form variants are scored as independent topics; the canonical full phrase accumulates fewer hits and collapses. CONFIRMED ERROR (verified, severity low): "belgium vs iran" (a real 6/21 World Cup fixture) reads 10.2/17.7 MONITORING — under-weight but explainable by a known, DOCUMENTED, fix-pending mechanism (CLAUDE.md sec 15 M/D reweighting names this exact case as IN DESIGN). Note: the auditor's proposed "wide positive gap" expectation here is wrong — a fully-arrived mainstream event should read HIGH on both axes, not with a wide gap; the auditor conflated mainstream with early-emerging. DATA-INTEGRITY BUG (basis: arithmetic): the stored heisenberg_gap contradicts Detection−Confidence on the entire mcp family (mcp 74.5−73.0 = +1.5 but stored gap −23; same on claude code, claude-code, model-context-protocol, mcp server) — a ~24pt internal contradiction breaking the "both scores explain each other" test, independent of the mainstream question. ACCURATE EXAMPLES (the engine genuinely works here): trump 94.6/94.2/+0.3, FIFA 93.7/93.7/0.0, iran 86.1/90.1/-4, election 94.7/94.3/+0.4, canada 90/91.7/-1.7, claude 77.8/86.2 STRONG — all high+high+aligned, the correct already-arrived read. NEGATIVE CONTROLS also pass: SpaceX IPO 2026 (24.9/25.4, n=0) and obscure dev-tool slugs (fastapi 46.2/48.6, langchain 45.5/47.9) sit LOW on BOTH axes — the engine does NOT fabricate confidence for niche topics, so the failure is ONE-DIRECTIONAL (it strips score from mid-mainstream topics; it does not invent it for niche ones). SATURATION/SEPARATION (basis: 2,103-topic distribution): the instrument is NOT collapsed — stage medians ladder monotonically (Detection 90.5/74.1/46.0/32.8/17.3), 520/595 distinct Detection/Confidence values exist, and the gap converges to ~0 only for genuinely-arrived BREAKOUT topics (median -0.8). Real caveats: heavy near-duplicate string variants score identically (inflating apparent compression and the catch-all), some single-platform GitHub slugs carry mid-range Detection on one source, and a few thin BREAKOUT/anomaly rows ("bonkers", 5 mentions/4 platforms, 86–91 Detection) warrant a corroboration check. HONEST SAMPLE/AGE CAVEAT: all of the above is a single-day snapshot (2026-06-22) over a 100-topic hand-checked sample within 2,103 topics; it is directionally decisive (it fails on the day's #1 event plus a reproducible 7-topic pattern) but is not a multi-week out-of-sample false-positive measurement, and the stated 22%/<9% FP rates remain design targets, not ledger-confirmed.


## market_signal_analysis

The Market Signal tier ladder (ELEVATED ≥80 / ACTIVE 60–80 / BUILDING 40–60 / ROUTINE 25–40 / DORMANT <25, on the Detection+Confidence average, with CALIBRATING overriding) is conceptually sound and the engine's baseline-relative design is genuinely strong: every component is scored as a z-score against the item's OWN history (z=0→0.30, z=2→~0.74), which correctly fixes the size-bias bug where mega-caps auto-scored ELEVATED on coverage volume alone. The CALIBRATING short-circuit is well-implemented and hard to bypass (a fresh IPO like SPCX trips all components and is honestly flagged "snapshot, not a confirmed signal"), which is exactly the cold-start honesty an institutional buyer should want. BUT as surfaced today the tier system does NOT track or separate instruments coherently. From the live 88-instrument pull (all fresh, scored within 0.7h — staleness is NOT the problem): the headline tier (market_gradient.tier / risk_stage) is ROUTINE for 87/88 (98.9%) — only alphabet is BUILDING — so the user-facing tier carries essentially ZERO information. THREE tier fields disagree on the same row: tier vs risk_stage vs classification (e.g. cre = ROUTINE tier but ELEVATED classification; recession, spacex, jpmorgan = ROUTINE tier but WATCH classification). There is no single source of truth. COLD-START CONTRADICTION: 28.4% of instruments are flagged calibrating=true, yet 0% have sufficient_baseline=false — every calibrating row simultaneously reports "enough baseline." The two gates are computed on different inputs (calibrating ≈ thin total_signals 1–6; sufficient_baseline ≈ cycle count) and are never reconciled. VALUE QUANTIZATION: Detection snaps to a few discrete buckets (20 instruments at 34.6, 16 at 32.2, 9 at 33.9), and calibrating rows collapse to floor pairs (29.9/29.9, sig=1) presented in the same shape as measured reads. ABNORMALITY IS NOISE-DOMINATED on thin coverage: the nine "highest abnormality" instruments (abn 4.8, delta 5.3%) are all 2-signal micro-cap floats (Lakewood Amedex, Boxlight, Utime) classed ROUTINE, while jpmorgan (abn 2.4, sig 125) is WATCH — so the "biggest movers" sort surfaces illiquid noise above real macro movers (recession, spacex). STRUCTURAL COVERAGE GAP: the tradable universe is a hardcoded ~16-name watchlist (15 mega-caps + SpaceX); anything off it silently zeroes positioning/fundamentals/momentum — the "SpaceX problem." PARITY RISK: two divergent market formulas coexist (the z-score engine that is actually persisted vs an absolute-magnitude inline variant tied to the Grade tool), so the Market tab and Grade tool can disagree for the same ticker — a CLAUDE.md 3-platform-parity violation. Also flagged: a hardcoded identical interpretation string across all instruments and double-encoded UTF-8 mojibake in the payload (will render as garbage on the terminal). NET: the engine is directionally honest about a quiet tape and its baseline-relativity is real, but the tiers as surfaced are uninformative and internally contradictory; "mostly ROUTINE" is defensible for a quiet market but is not the same as a tier system that is tracked accurately and surfaced coherently.


## benchmarks_and_calibration

The no-gap test currently has no objective ground truth — it was scored by hand against WebSearch. The fix is a free, non-circular external anchor, and the clear winner is the WIKIPEDIA / WIKIMEDIA PAGEVIEWS API (AQS REST v1). It is the textbook attention ground-truth in published research, it is genuinely FREE, it is licensed CC0 1.0 (public domain — the cleanest possible position for a commercial product to ingest and even resurface), and it is FULLY non-circular: pageviews come from Wikimedia's own server logs, an independent population on a platform NowTrendIn does not collect from, sharing ZERO inputs with the Gradient Score (no RSS, no X scan, no blog feeds, no Detection/Confidence components). For the no-gap test it maps almost directly: an already-mainstream topic reliably has high, STABLE article traffic; a niche/pre-arrival topic has thin or no traffic. Pair it with GDELT DOC 2.0 (TimelineVol, free, percent-of-global-coverage) as an independent MEDIA-SUPPLY second axis — true mainstream should be HIGH on BOTH the demand axis (Wikipedia) and the supply axis (GDELT); disagreement is itself diagnostic (high GDELT + low Wikipedia = media-pushed, no public pull). Two critical exclusions on integrity grounds: X/Twitter is DISQUALIFIED as an anchor because it is already a scoring INPUT (the 6h scan) — using it as ground truth is the exact circular-metric failure the integrity standard forbids; and Google Trends must stay in its EXISTING lane as the breakout/arrival-timing anchor in the accuracy ledger — reusing it for no-gap would double-anchor to the same provider's attention definition and over-fit. Reddit (paid ~$12k/mo, opaque, and the wrong/early construct) and Google Ngram (annual, years-lagged, retrospective-only) are not live anchors. CONCRETE NON-CIRCULAR CALIBRATION LOOP (weekly, mirroring the accuracy-ledger cadence): STEP 0 — hard firewall: Wikipedia/GDELT data is written ONLY to a dedicated held-out table (attention_anchor) that NO Gradient-Score component, calibration weight, dual-pathway gate, or serve_payload ever reads. STEP 1 — resolve each scored topic to a canonical Wikipedia article (title-normalize + search; unresolved topics QUARANTINE to a review queue, never guessed — same condition-precedent discipline as the ingestion gate; this also directly attacks the surface-form fragmentation bug). STEP 2 — pull trailing ~90d daily pageviews + GDELT coverage-share; compute mainstream-LEVEL = log10(median daily views), STABILITY = low coefficient of variation, and coverage-share level. STEP 3 — label ALREADY-MAINSTREAM = pageviews above a high corpus percentile (~p80) AND stable AND high GDELT, EARLY = low+rising. STEP 4 — score the no-gap test as a confusion matrix; the key failure metric is FALSE-EARLY (we called something emerging that the anchors say is already mainstream — precisely the Greenspan/country-collapse failure). STEP 5 — calibrate DIRECTION only: a rising false-early rate prompts a HUMAN-reviewed adjustment of the SATURATION/ESTABLISHED-discount thresholds; the external number is never regressed into a weight. STEP 6 — a WORSENING drift alarm (like catchall_floor_log) trips when false-early climbs past a set delta. This stays permanently non-circular because the anchors live in a held-out table the score cannot read, share no inputs with the score, are never auto-fit, and are kept on a different external series from the breakout (Trends) job.

## verdict

Not yet — NowTrendIn cannot today make an unqualified "measures where human attention is moving before it arrives" claim to an institutional buyer, because the instrument fails its own no-gap credibility floor on the day's single most important confirmed event (Greenspan, +16.2 anomaly gap) and on a systematic class of already-arrived mainstream topics that collapse to a ~45/67 EMERGING signature, while the moat's lead claim is currently UNPROVEN and at last ledger CONTRADICTED (0.0% honest hit rate). What the product CAN defend right now is real and not trivial: a structurally honest dual-score design faithfully implemented in code, a verified no-N-into-Gradient firewall (the core integrity rule), genuinely baseline-relative market scoring with disciplined cold-start honesty, clean monotonic separation across 2,103 topics, demonstrably correct already-arrived reads for the famous top tier (trump/FIFA/iran/election), and a one-directional failure mode that never fabricates confidence for niche topics. For this to become a defensible "human attention" instrument, the following must be true: the heisenberg_gap and surface-form-fragmentation bugs are fixed; the dual-pathway anti-collapse protection extends across the full mainstream band; a held-out, non-circular Wikipedia+GDELT no-gap anchor is wired and shows acceptable false-early precision; the market tier fields and cold-start flags are reconciled into a single coherent surface; and the accuracy ledger clears its own published success bar so the "before it arrives" lead is denominator-backed rather than a claim. Until then it is a promising, honestly-architected pre-validation instrument — sellable as a measurement tool with explicit caveats, but not yet as a proven attention-prediction moat.
