# NowTrendIn Plain-English Glossary

> Board condition N6 (Outsider memo, 2026-07-17 assessor gate): every house term in one
> plain sentence, so no number ever needs dialect to be understood. Internal reference —
> keep client-facing copy consistent with these definitions.

**Gradient Score (Detection)** — how strongly attention is moving toward a topic right now,
measured from outside data only.

**Confidence** — how broadly independent, credible sources confirm that same movement.

**Gap** — the distance between Detection and Confidence; a big gap means movement that
confirmation hasn't caught up with yet.

**N (Platform Indicator)** — how often our own platform surfaces or tracks the topic. It is
never part of the Gradient Score and never validates it (that would be circular).

**Dark Matter (D)** — early attention in expert and niche channels before mainstream media
covers it.

**Mainstream (M)** — attention breadth across established, reputable outlets.

**Accuracy Ledger** — the tamper-evident scorecard: every detection is time-stamped when made,
then judged later against independent outside data. History is never deleted or edited.

**Held-out** — data or checks used only to judge the system, never to tune it; the judge and
the student are never the same data.

**LED** — a race we won: our detection came first and Google Trends broke out later.

**Same-day** — we and Google Trends surfaced it on the same day; a tie, not a win.

**Lagged (near-miss)** — a race we ran and lost: Google broke out first, within the grace
window of our first sighting.

**Pre-broken** — it was already big before we ever saw it — a race we never got to run;
it measures how fast we discover topics, not whether we predict them.

**False positive** — a detection where no outside breakout ever arrived within the full
365-day patience window. (Zero today is guaranteed by construction — no row is old enough
to have timed out yet.)

**Blended hit rate** — wins divided by EVERYTHING resolved, including races we never got to
run. The most conservative number we track.

**Tracked-race rate** — wins divided by races actually run (LED + same-day + near-miss).
THE headline accuracy number, always quoted with its sample size and interval.

**Wilson interval** — the honest range around a rate given its sample size; on small samples,
most of the number is interval.

**Epoch (v1/v2 engine)** — which engine generation made the detection; rates are always
readable per epoch so a blended figure can't hide a change in the machine.

**Patience window (365d)** — a pending detection gets a full year for attention to arrive
before it can be judged a miss.

**Shadow check** — a new monitoring check that reports but is excluded from grades until it
proves itself against a naive baseline.

**Catch-all %** — the share of topics that fall into the generic category bucket; a congestion
gauge only, never an accuracy figure.

**Referee (Wikipedia)** — an independent, free outside data source (pageviews) used to
corroborate wins and cross-check looks; it diagnoses, it never tunes.

**Drift alarm** — a standing series tracking how closely our scores rank-correlate with a
lagging fame index; RISING correlation is a warning (we detect before, not after), so the
goal is disagreement now, agreement later.
