The Market Gradient / Market Signal engine **measures** signal state (Detection = leading/analyst+positioning, Confidence = hard fundamentals+price, gap = how early). It does NOT and should NOT encode macro human-judgment factors. Those are a **monitoring layer the founder applies on top**.

**The founder's worked example (Microsoft, 2026-06-10)** — keep as the template for that layer: Microsoft reads high Detection / low Confidence (emerging software strength, analysts/positioning ahead of confirmed fundamentals). Whether its price actually jumps depends on human-judgment factors the gradient can't measure:
- the **hardware market appearing to peak** (AI hardware cycle topping)
- the **upcoming SpaceX IPO** and whether it + other **AI IPOs hold price or bleed on profit-taking**

**How to apply:** When discussing or extending the Market Gradient, treat these macro/event factors as *context the user monitors alongside the score*, never as components to bake into Detection/Confidence (that would re-introduce subjectivity into a measurement instrument — see [[feedback-integrity-standard]]). If the user wants these surfaced, do it as a separate "monitoring notes / watch factors" annotation, clearly distinct from the measured score. The gradient says "leading signals ahead of confirmation"; the human decides what that means given the cycle. Related: [[feedback-no-circular-metrics]].
